Description

Pesto is a classic Italian sauce originating from Genoa, in the Liguria region of Italy. Traditionally made by crushing fresh basil leaves, garlic, pine nuts, Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese, and extra virgin olive oil using a mortar and pestle, pesto offers a vibrant, aromatic blend of flavors. The name “pesto” comes from the Italian word pestare, meaning “to pound” or “to crush,” reflecting its original preparation method.

Today, pesto is widely used as a versatile condiment, most commonly served with pasta, but also spread on sandwiches, drizzled over roasted vegetables, or stirred into soups. While the classic Genovese version remains the most popular, modern variations use ingredients like arugula, sun-dried tomatoes, or walnuts to create unique twists on this fresh and savory sauce.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh basil leaves (packed)
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmigiano-Reggiano (or vegan alternative)
  • Salt to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper (optional)

Steps

  1. Toast the pine nuts:Lightly toast the pine nuts in a dry pan over medium heat for 2-3 minutes until golden, stirring often. Let cool.
  2. Blend the ingredients:In a food processor or blender, combine basil, garlic, and pine nuts. Pulse a few times to chop.
  3. Add oil and cheese:With the processor running, slowly drizzle in the olive oil until smooth. Stir in the grated cheese.
  4. Season:Add salt and pepper to taste. Adjust the consistency with more olive oil if needed.
  5. Serve:Toss with hot pasta, spread on bread, or use as a sauce for roasted vegetables.
