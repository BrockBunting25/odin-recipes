Description

Vegan carbonara is a plant-based twist on the traditional Italian pasta dish, known for its rich and creamy texture. While classic carbonara uses eggs, cheese, and pancetta, this vegan version relies on cashews, plant milk, and nutritional yeast to create a velvety sauce full of umami flavor. Smoked paprika and miso paste help to mimic the savory depth of the original dish, while optional vegan bacon or smoked tempeh adds a satisfying, smoky bite.

This dairy-free and egg-free carbonara is comforting, flavorful, and simple to prepare, making it a perfect choice for weeknight dinners or special occasions. Tossed with your favorite pasta and finished with a generous grind of black pepper, it’s a satisfying take on an Italian classic—no animal products needed.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup raw cashews (soaked for 4 hours or boiled for 10 minutes)
  • 1 ¼ cups unsweetened plant milk (almond, oat, or soy)
  • 3 tablespoons nutritional yeast
  • 1 tablespoon white miso paste (for umami)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika (for a subtle bacon-like flavor)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional: vegan bacon or smoked tempeh, chopped and crisped

Steps

  1. Blend the sauce:Drain the soaked cashews and blend them with plant milk, nutritional yeast, miso paste, onion powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper until smooth and creamy.
  2. Cook garlic:In a pan over medium heat, sauté minced garlic in olive oil for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Heat the sauce:Pour the blended sauce into the pan with the garlic. Simmer gently for 2–3 minutes, stirring until heated through and thickened slightly.
  4. Add vegan bacon (optional)If using, stir in crisped vegan bacon or smoked tempeh at this point.
  5. Serve:Toss immediately with cooked pasta and finish with extra black pepper on top.
